How Your Donation Will Help

Every blood donation has the power to save lives and make a meaningful impact. Here's how your contribution helps:

Save Lives: Your blood can save up to three lives, providing hope to those in emergencies.

Support Medical Treatments: Blood is vital for surgeries, cancer treatments, and managing chronic diseases.

Community Strength: Your donation fosters a stronger, healthier community by ensuring blood availability.

Emergency Readiness: Blood reserves help hospitals respond to unexpected crises and natural disasters.

Are You Eligible to Donate Blood?

Donating blood is a generous act that saves lives, but there are some requirements to ensure your safety and the safety of recipients. Check the guidelines below:

Eligibility Requirements

  • Must be at least 18 years old and weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs).
  • In good general health and feeling well.
  • No major surgeries or blood transfusions in the last 6 months.
  • Not currently pregnant or nursing (if applicable).
  • Free from cold, flu, or any active infections.
  • Not traveled to malaria-endemic areas in the past 6 months.

Who Should Avoid Donating?

  • If you have tested positive for HIV or other transmissible diseases.
  • If you are on certain medications (consult your doctor).
  • If you’ve had a tattoo or piercing within the last 4 months.
  • If you feel unwell on the day of donation.

If you're unsure, contact your local blood donation center for personalized advice.
